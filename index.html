The read

Two products live inside the Resideline app, and they sit on different assumptions.

You built a platform that tells investors what a property rents, sells, or flips for, in one report. The bulk-analysis feature (500 properties from CSV in seconds) is the real unlock. The resideline.com/resideline-api page says you are already thinking past the single-investor customer toward the PropTech builders who need your AVM as a feed. That is a second product living inside the first one.

Here is how we would look at the stack specifically. Roughly 60% of what makes the three-strategy report work is traditional code and database: MLS ingestion, comp joins, cost tables, rent seasonality math. Roughly 30% is rule-based logic: which comps qualify, which cost rule applies, which exit strategy is eligible for a given property type. The last 10% is the genuine AI layer: valuation synthesis, confidence calibration, and the judgment calls on thin markets.

Most PropTech shops get this ratio backwards, put an LLM where a SQL query would do, and watch their infra cost eat the $99 margin. The orchestration pattern keeps each layer honest and each cost centered, so you can tell which call path ran on a given deal.

Three strategies, three engineering problems

One endpoint, three comp sets, three cost models.

Flip, LTR, and STR each need different comps, different cost rules, and different confidence bands. The "instantly" promise at $99 pricing means the unit economics quietly depend on whichever call path is cheapest, not the one the user needs.

Flip

ARV and rehab, comp-driven.

Needs sold comps within 90 days, renovation cost table keyed to zip and finish level. Cost drift shows up fastest here.

Risk: stale rehab tables → margin leak.
Long-term rent

Rent comp, not sale comp.

Different comp set. Lease data is sparser than sale data. Vacancy and concession assumptions tend to live in code, not in the UI.

Risk: assumption drift hidden from the user.
Short-term rent

Seasonality, occupancy, cost.

Thinnest comp set. Most volatile cost model. After any MLS or booking-feed change, STR accuracy drifts quietly for a week before anyone notices.

Risk: silent STR drift after upstream change.
